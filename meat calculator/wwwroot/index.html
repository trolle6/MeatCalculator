What this model does (and does not)
  • Does: Estimate collagen render % and hot-hold hours from pull temp, hold temp, and time — a planning aid for pull-and-hold brisket.
  • Does not: Replace a probe in the flat, bark feel, or your cooker’s quirks. USDA grade is scored on the ribeye, not the brisket sticker alone.
  • Does not: Guarantee slice timing — point vs flat, wrap, and cambro heat vary. Compare presets; trust probe and butter-glide feel at ~82 °C (180 °F)+.
  • Does not: Know your pit — hold hours are modeled; your hot box may run hotter, colder, or faster. Use Juicy vs Hotter as brackets, then adjust on the cook.

If numbers feel off, say what you expected on GitHub Issues — patterns matter more than one report.

Evaporative stall

Between 65.5 – 74 °C internal the meat sweats and cools itself — bump pit temp or airflow to power through, or moisture keeps leaving.

Smoke past 140 °F

Smoke flavour and ring development do not stop at 60 °C (140 °F). Ring can keep building toward ~76.5 °C (170 °F) on a wet bark.

Probe the flat

From ~82 °C (180 °F), you want glide-in butter on the flat — not mush, not cold resistance.
